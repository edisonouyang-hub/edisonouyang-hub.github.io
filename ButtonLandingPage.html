Have you ever wanted to be a lizard?

Unfortunately, to be a lizard is to be like Sisyphus, ever catching the round object of its desires, hunting down the next high, only to probably fail and be crushed by its own hubris. But still the lizard craves to win; they demand to show the forces that confine it to its limitations what its true heights are. The lizard persists, even if the game was made to spite their existence and to mock their indulgence. The lizard knows what it likes and how to get it and thus why it dies and is reborn: to climb the tower and reach the end. No matter what the statistics say, no matter the lack of a safety net, no matter what is lost on the way to ascension, it persists. It persists because it knows it must, for there is literally nothing else to do but to be falsely content with nothing to do. And why is the high achieved when the lizard finds itself at the top of the mountain, having picked the fruit of its labor that the gods denied it? What more is there for the lizard to do but ultimately decide whether or not it is content to give up or reach once more a new height of different adversities?
