gLitch

Washed by Nostalgia.

Turning my old artwork into a raw photoshop file I was able to put it through Audacity to distort the art and in turn the meaning of it. To my memory I had added 2 fade ins and a fade out to show a fast forward to the present through an ending of 2 periods in my life. Past high school when I had created the source material, past college to the point which I take this class, to the current era where I am close to graduating we continue the story behind the art. What we encounter is a branched evolution of my thought process.

The meaning of the glitch that I had selected is similar to that of a glitch one would see on an old TV. It's an old memory, an outdated thought that I once had. To me it's almost a nostalgic yet fading memory of an attitude I had once. All children are nihilists; what that meant was that no child is born with the knowledge of faith or philosophy, and thus live without any understanding but only for what is in front of them. However, if I were to reevaluate my current beliefs, I'd instead say all children are absurdist, more wise than they understand, but not so wise that they could be distressed by their thoughts. To some degree before my fresh wounds from newly learnt lessons, perhaps it wasn't children, but my still immature self in high school that highlight the nihilism the original piece sought to discuss.

2d meMe

The Title of this piece is "TS Central Recap" It's a love letter to a friend group I'm in and the channel we co own together. It is as if a montage of all the progress we made on this one platform we share.

I took multiple screenshots of videos, livestreams, and shorts of the Youtube Channel "Team Scrub." I layered them over each other at low opacity and on top I put a screenshot of Metal Gear Solid 4's end cutscene where Solid Snake and Liquid Ocelot fight each other on top. I took Kojima's face mashed with Absolute Cinema meme, and the man pointing meme and friends cheering meme to represent the one person who is watching multiple people. To further demonstrate the context of the the meme, gave it the visual aesthetics of a Youtube Thumbnail, with the correct ratio, the watch time bar, and the video length.

You can pick up the meaning of me and my friend's enjoyment of both playing games and watching stories together, but also you can see us boasting about the videos we've made. The man pointing meme represents how we really have fewer viewers than we do people working on the channel represented by the friends cheering meme; with it lined up in this order you can read a story about the content it is making a comment on. Each image I feel provides a little more context about our experiences as friends and our collective projects.

time-based mashup

This is my Yugioh Catchup. It is a mashup of many videos that discuss the card game know as Yugioh. The work is intended to not only inform the watcher about the game in 2 minutes, it also comments on the people who produce the game bringing up news and history of where the game is coming from.

I have played card games such as and especially Yugioh since I have been a child and I've grown up along side how fast the game has grown. I found the videos I've used in this mashup through happening to cross them as I've been getting back into the game from a few years ago. I always hear about how hard it is to get into this game I love because of how complicated it is to play and maintain as a hobby. I don't really seek to convince anyone with this video to play Yugioh, but I do wish to comment and reflect the sentiments of both fans and nonfans have to say about my favorite game. I must admit that this video requires a bit of knowledge about card games in general and to recall some nostalgia about Yugioh to understand the ending.

My mashup is about comparing Yugioh with another card game, against its own flaws, and asking people to examine the game with all the information laid out. It has reasons for why this game is bad, some reasons for why you might enjoy it, and it reflects a hobby that is near and dear to me and many who play it. But I also made it to warn about the tragedy that surrounds the game, the bad reputation that it has, ranging from how confusing it is to get into, how liable you are to get scammed, and how companies and copyright always are against you as a consumer. It is a commentary against a hobby that you love, and acknowledgement that you have to understand that what you like, comes with baggage.

The HyperText

Pretend to Play a little game with me.

I've always been enraptured by games and stories that have multiple endings as determined by the audience. I hoped to give at least a few couple of endings for replay ability. I was inspired by such media as the "Henry Stickman Games" or choose your own adventure books, where you encounter various ways to win and lose the game. It's not advanced, but neither is this kind of story telling's game mechanics. I give a nod to my favorite choose your adventure games such as Dungeons and Dragons or Divinity with the dice roll section. It's an ode to stories I've experienced while playing tabletop games with friends. In the end, whether you choose to click the button in accordance to the roll of the die or not speaks to the genre, where truth of the roll may be obscured or brutally accepted for better story telling.
